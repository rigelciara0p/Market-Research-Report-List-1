<p><p>The microelectronics plating market encompasses various metal types used for component protection and functionality. Gold plating offers excellent conductivity and corrosion resistance, making it ideal for high-performance applications. Zinc plating provides cost-effective corrosion protection. Nickel is commonly used as a barrier layer due to its hardness and resistance to oxidation. Bronze plating enhances thermal and electrical conductivity. Tin plating protects against corrosion while ensuring good solderability. Copper plating is favored for its excellent electrical properties, while "Others" may include specialty coatings and alloys.</p></p>
