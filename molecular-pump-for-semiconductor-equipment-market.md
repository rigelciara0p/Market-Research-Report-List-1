<p><p>The semiconductor equipment market features various molecular pump types, including magnetic levitation, oil lubricated, and grease lubricated pumps. Magnetic levitation molecular pumps utilize magnetic fields for frictionless operation, enhancing reliability and efficiency. Oil lubricated molecular pumps use oil to reduce friction, providing improved performance and longevity, but may require more maintenance. Grease lubricated molecular pumps, on the other hand, utilize grease for lubrication, often found in less demanding applications due to their simpler design and lower cost, but with limited lifespan.</p></p>
