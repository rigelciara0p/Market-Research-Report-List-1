<p><p>The laboratory rheometer market includes various types, each serving distinct applications. Dynamic rheometers assess materials' flow and deformation behaviors under oscillatory motion, ideal for characterizing viscoelastic properties. Capillary rheometers measure viscosity by forcing fluids through narrow channels, useful for polymers and food products. Torque rheometers evaluate material viscosity under steady shear by measuring the torque required, often used for high-viscosity substances. Additional rheometer types cater to specialized applications, ensuring diverse solutions for research and industrial testing.</p></p>
